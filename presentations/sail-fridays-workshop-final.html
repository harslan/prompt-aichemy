Stage 1: Basic Prompt

The student who treats this like simple homework:

Analyze the Boston Airbnb listings data to identify factors that distinguish successful hosts from struggling ones. Focus on review scores and pricing.

What's Missing:

  • No definition of what "success" means
  • No role framing or business context
  • No specific stakeholders mentioned
  • No request for critical evaluation
  • Just "analyze and tell me what correlates"
Typical Result: AI returns obvious patterns‚Äî"High reviews = success, Higher prices indicate better listings, Superhosts perform well." True, but surface-level. A student who stops here gets a C.

Stage 2A: Adding Strategic Context

First iteration: Add role framing and multi-dimensional success

You are a data strategist for Airbnb's Host Success team. Develop a multi-dimensional framework for "host success": 1. Financial Performance (revenue, pricing, occupancy) 2. Guest Satisfaction (reviews, response, engagement) 3. Platform Health (quality, neighborhood reputation) 4. Host Sustainability (burnout indicators, balance) Analyze Boston listings across these dimensions.

What Changed:

  • ‚úÖ Strategic role creates business context
  • ‚úÖ Success defined across multiple dimensions
  • ‚úÖ Considers platform health, not just revenue
SAIL Activation: Social Intelligence (multiple stakeholders) + AI Literacy (role-based prompting)

Stage 2B: Adding Critical Investigation

Second iteration: Add the investigation request and ethical lens

SPECIFIC INVESTIGATION: Superhosts are Airbnb's elite tier. Analyze: - Do they charge MORE or LESS than similar listings? - If less, why? Strategic volume play? Complacency? - Do guests rate them HIGHER or LOWER on value? CRITICAL EVALUATION: - Question correlation vs. causation - Consider what data does NOT tell us - Acknowledge potential biases - Think about second-order effects OUTPUT: Success framework, superhost analysis, ethical considerations, actionable recommendations

Additional Depth:

  • ‚úÖ Directs investigation of surprising patterns
  • ‚úÖ Requests critical questioning of outputs
  • ‚úÖ Embeds ethical awareness naturally
  • ‚úÖ Demands actionable strategy
Full SAIL: Innovation/Inquiry (question assumptions) + Leadership (ethical implications)

The Design Pattern That Works

What made the Airbnb assignment activate SAIL naturally?

1. Professional Role Framing

"You are a consultant for Airbnb's Boston Market Development team..."

Students aren't doing homework‚Äîthey're solving a real business problem.

2. Authentic Complexity

Real data with surprises + Competing stakeholder interests + No single "right answer"

Hosts want income, neighborhoods want affordability, guests want quality, city wants compliance.

3. Strategic Questions

Define success multi-dimensionally ‚Üí Investigate surprising patterns ‚Üí Balance competing interests

Can't answer without Social Intelligence, AI Literacy, Innovation/Inquiry, and Leadership.
