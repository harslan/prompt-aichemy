SAIL-Based Competition Design Template

Designing AI-Enabled Learning Experiences That Develop Strategic Judgment

SAIL Fridays • November 14, 2025 • Prof. Hasan Arslan

The Core Template

Title: [Your Domain] AIChemy Dataset: Real, complex, current data from your domain Role: "You are a [professional consultant] hired by [organization]..." Question 1 - Technical/Analytical: What patterns distinguish X from Y? (e.g., successful vs. struggling, high-performing vs. low-performing) Question 2 - Strategic/Systemic: Which [segments/markets/areas] show healthy conditions vs. concerning trends? What are growth implications? Question 3 - Ethical/Stakeholder: What drives [quality/satisfaction/outcomes], and how do we balance competing interests? Deliverables: Report + Presentation + Prompt Documentation Assessment: Business value + Prompt quality + SAIL integration + Communication

Four Design Principles

1. Authentic Complexity: Use real data with real stakeholders and real ethical tensions. Students can't optimize for one metric—they must navigate complexity (like real business).
2. Role-Based Framing: Students aren't "doing a project"—they're consultants. This shifts mindset from "what does professor want?" to "what does client need?"
3. Structured Ambiguity: Clear requirements (address all questions, document prompts), but multiple paths to excellence. Grade reasoning, not "right answers."
4. SAIL Integration: Don't force SAIL as a checklist—design experiences where all four pillars emerge naturally from doing good work.

SAIL Framework Quick Reference

S - Social Intelligence

"How do we connect?"

Translate technical insights for diverse audiences

A - AI Literacy

"How does it work?"

Strategic AI tool direction & understanding

I - Innovation/Inquiry

"What's wrong/missing?"

Critical thinking beyond AI limitations

L - Leadership

"Should we do this?"

Ethical decisions about AI implementation

Assessment Rubric

  • Business Value (30%): Actionability, strategic thinking, impact
  • Prompt Engineering (30%): Sophistication, iteration, strategic approach
  • SAIL Framework (20%): Natural integration of all four pillars
  • Presentation (20%): Clarity, storytelling, visual communication

Domain-Specific Examples

Finance - Portfolio AIChemy: Consultant for university endowment analyzing 20 years of returns. Q1: What distinguishes resilient portfolios? Q2: When do correlations break? Q3: How to balance returns with ESG?
Marketing - Brand AIChemy: Brand strategist for Boston business with social media + sales data. Q1: What drives conversion vs. awareness? Q2: What are behavioral tribes? Q3: How to use AI without losing authenticity?
Accounting - Audit AIChemy: Audit consultant with transaction data. Q1: What patterns suggest fraud vs. errors? Q2: Where should auditors focus time? Q3: What requires human vs. AI judgment?
Key Insight: All examples share real data complexity, multiple stakeholder perspectives, ethical tensions, and communication challenges across audiences. The template works because it creates conditions where SAIL pillars emerge naturally from doing good work.
