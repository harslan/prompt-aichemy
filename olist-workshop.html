ADVANCED PROMPT ENGINEERING TECHNIQUES

Strategic Persona Assignment

Go beyond basic role assignment to create detailed personas with specific expertise, perspectives, and objectives.

"Take on the persona of an e-commerce operations director with 15 years of experience in Brazilian retail markets and a specialization in supply chain optimization. Your key objective is to maximize seller efficiency while maintaining delivery quality..."

Multi-round Insight Refinement

Structure your analysis as a conversational process that builds depth through sequential prompts.

"First round: Identify the top 3 anomalies in the Olist delivery data. Second round: For each anomaly, develop a hypothesis about root causes. Third round: Design experiments to test each hypothesis..."

Counterfactual Reasoning

Ask the AI to explore alternative scenarios and outcomes to reveal deeper insights.

"Based on the customer satisfaction analysis, what would likely happen to Olist's NPS score if delivery times improved by 20%? Conversely, what would be the impact if they worsened by 20%?"

Synthetic Subject Matter Experts

Create a panel of virtual experts to analyze data from multiple disciplinary perspectives.

"Analyze the Olist review data through the lenses of: a consumer psychologist, a data scientist specializing in NLP, and a Brazilian e-commerce marketing specialist. Identify where their perspectives align and diverge."

Constraint-driven Innovation

Use specific constraints to drive more creative and focused analysis.

"Identify strategies to improve Olist's seller performance that: 1) require zero additional technology investment, 2) can be implemented within 30 days, and 3) focus exclusively on process optimization."

First Principles Decomposition

Break complex questions down to fundamental components before building up solutions.

"First, define what constitutes 'seller success' for Olist based on the available metrics. Then identify the minimal set of factors that contribute to these success indicators. Finally, analyze how these factors interact."

OPTIMIZING PROMPTS: BEFORE & AFTER

Example 1: Customer Satisfaction Analysis

Basic Prompt

Analyze what factors affect customer satisfaction in the Olist data.

Issues: Too vague, lacks context, doesn't specify metrics or deliverables.

Optimized Prompt

Role: You are a customer experience analytics specialist with expertise in Brazilian e-commerce platforms. Context: Olist is experiencing variable customer satisfaction scores across different regions and product categories. Management needs to understand the key drivers of satisfaction to implement targeted improvements. Task: Analyze the correlation between review scores (from olist_order_reviews_dataset) and other variables including: 1. Delivery time (estimated vs. actual) 2. Product categories 3. Price points 4. Seller response time 5. Geographic location Output: Provide the following: 1. Identification of the top 3 factors with strongest correlation to review scores 2. Visualization of satisfaction trends over time (2016-2018) 3. Specific recommendations for improving satisfaction in the lowest-performing segment 4. Dashboard-ready metrics for ongoing customer satisfaction monitoring Constraints: - Focus only on orders with complete data across all relevant datasets - Prioritize actionable insights over general observations - Consider potential seasonal effects in your analysis

Example 2: Seller Performance Optimization

Basic Prompt

What makes a good seller on Olist? How can they improve?

Issues: Ambiguous metrics, lacks business context, no clear deliverable.

Optimized Prompt

I want you to take on the role of Olist's Chief Seller Success Officer, responsible for optimizing the performance of the marketplace's 3,000+ sellers. Background: Olist's business model depends on successful sellers, but we've observed high variance in seller performance. Top-performing sellers (top 10% by revenue) significantly outperform others, and we want to understand why. Analysis Request: Using the olist_sellers_dataset, olist_order_items_dataset, and olist_order_reviews_dataset: 1. Define a comprehensive "Seller Success Score" based on: - Revenue generation - Customer satisfaction (review scores) - Order fulfillment time - Repeat purchase rate for their products 2. Identify the distinguishing characteristics of top-quartile sellers versus bottom-quartile sellers, focusing on: - Geographic location - Product category focus - Pricing strategies - Customer service indicators (response time, issue resolution) 3. Develop a "Seller Success Playbook" that includes: - 5 specific, data-backed recommendations for low-performing sellers - Implementation timeline with prioritized actions - Expected impact quantification for each recommendation - Simple A/B test design to validate the effectiveness of recommendations Format your analysis as an executive presentation with supporting data visualizations, focusing on actionable insights rather than raw data. Ethical consideration: Ensure recommendations don't disadvantage smaller sellers or those from less developed regions of Brazil.

REAL-WORLD OLIST CASE STUDY

Geographic Market Expansion Strategy

Business Challenge

Olist's executive team wants to identify the most promising geographic regions for targeted marketing and seller recruitment efforts to expand their marketplace presence.

Available Data Elements

  • Customer geolocation data
  • Seller geolocation data
  • Order volume and value by region
  • Delivery performance metrics
  • Product category performance by region

Business Objectives

  • Identify underserved high-potential markets
  • Balance growth opportunity with operational feasibility
  • Develop region-specific seller recruitment strategies
  • Create targeted marketing approaches for each priority region

Multi-stage Prompt Strategy

Stage 1: Market Opportunity Analysis

Role: You are a market expansion strategist for Olist with expertise in Brazilian regional economics. Task: Analyze the Olist geolocation and order datasets to identify the top 5 underserved regions with high growth potential. Approach: 1. Calculate the current customer density and seller density by state and municipality 2. Identify regions with high customer density but low seller representation 3. Analyze average order value and purchase frequency in these regions 4. Evaluate delivery logistics performance to these areas 5. Consider product category preferences in each region Output: - Ranked list of top 5 opportunity regions with supporting metrics - Visual heat map showing the opportunity distribution across Brazil - Comparison table of key metrics for each identified region

Stage 2: Expansion Roadmap Development

Based on your previous market opportunity analysis, develop a 6-month expansion roadmap for the identified high-potential regions. For each region, provide: 1. Seller recruitment targets (number and product categories) 2. Customer acquisition strategy based on regional preferences 3. Logistics considerations and potential partnerships 4. Success metrics and KPIs for measuring expansion effectiveness 5. Risk assessment and mitigation strategies Structure this as a phased implementation plan with specific milestones, resource requirements, and expected outcomes at 30, 90, and 180 days.

Key Insight From This Case

Notice how the prompt strategy breaks down a complex business problem into sequential analyses. This multi-stage approach allows for deeper insights by building on previous findings, rather than trying to solve everything in one prompt.

THE ART OF DATA STORYTELLING WITH AI

From Insights to Narratives

The most powerful business insights are those embedded within compelling narratives that drive action. Here's how to craft prompts that transform data into stories:

Elements of Effective Data Storytelling

  • Clear Protagonist: Who is affected by the insight? (customers, sellers, etc.)
  • Conflict/Challenge: What problem is revealed by the data?
  • Journey: How did the situation evolve over time?
  • Resolution: What actions can address the challenge?
  • Stakes: Why does this matter to the business?

Storytelling Framework

Structure your prompts to guide the AI toward building a narrative arc:

  1. Situation: What is the current state?
  2. Complication: What challenge does the data reveal?
  3. Question: What needs to be understood or resolved?
  4. Answer: What insight addresses the question?
  5. Impact: What is the business significance?

Storytelling Prompt Example

Role: You are a business storyteller specializing in transforming e-commerce data into compelling narratives that drive executive decision-making. Task: Create a data-driven narrative about the customer journey on Olist's marketplace that will help executives understand critical friction points. Narrative Structure: 1. Begin with a typical Brazilian customer persona based on the most common demographics in the dataset 2. Trace this customer's journey from product search to post-purchase experience 3. Identify the key "moments of truth" where the data shows satisfaction is most at risk 4. Highlight how these moments differ across product categories and price points 5. Present the business impact of improving each critical touchpoint Format: - Craft a 5-minute narrative presentation - Include 3-5 supporting data visualizations that highlight key patterns - Provide a "narrative scorecard" that executives can use to track improvements in the customer journey - Balance quantitative metrics with qualitative insights Remember to ground every element of the narrative in the actual Olist data while making it relatable and actionable for business leaders.

Why This Works

This prompt transforms cold data analysis into a human-centered story with characters, challenges, and resolution opportunities. It creates an emotional connection to the insights while maintaining analytical rigor.

STRATEGIC INSIGHT EXTRACTION

Beyond Basic Analysis

The difference between good and exceptional prompts is the ability to extract strategic insights that drive business transformation, not just descriptive analysis.

Levels of Business Intelligence

  1. Descriptive: What happened? (Basic)
  2. Diagnostic: Why did it happen? (Intermediate)
  3. Predictive: What might happen next? (Advanced)
  4. Prescriptive: What should we do about it? (Strategic)

Insight Elevation Framework

For each business finding, prompt the AI to elevate the insight through these questions:

  • So what? Why does this matter to Olist?
  • Compared to what? How does this compare to industry benchmarks or previous periods?
  • Says who? What is the reliability of this finding?
  • Now what? What specific actions should follow?

Strategic Insight Prompt Example

Role: You are a strategic business consultant hired by Olist's executive team to identify transformational opportunities. Context: Olist has achieved steady growth but is seeking step-change improvements that will significantly outperform competitors and create sustainable advantages. Task: Using the Olist dataset, conduct a strategic analysis that goes beyond descriptive statistics to identify: 1. Hidden Opportunity Spaces: - Identify underserved market segments or product categories with high growth potential - Discover anomalous patterns that suggest untapped operational efficiencies - Detect emerging customer behavior trends before they become obvious 2. Competitive Advantage Analysis: - Determine Olist's current strengths compared to typical e-commerce marketplaces - Identify operational capabilities that could be developed into distinctive competencies - Suggest how Olist's unique Brazilian market position could be leveraged globally 3. Strategic Recommendation Portfolio: - Develop 3-5 high-impact strategic initiatives ranked by: * Expected business impact (quantified where possible) * Implementation feasibility * Time-to-value * Sustainability of competitive advantage Format your analysis as a strategic briefing that includes: - Executive summary of transformational opportunities - Supporting data visualizations that reveal non-obvious insights - Implementation roadmap with clear milestones and metrics - Risk assessment and mitigation strategies Approach this task by first identifying patterns in the data, then diagnosing root causes, then predicting future implications, and finally prescribing strategic actions.

Remember

The goal is not to have the AI simply analyze what's in the data, but to help you identify what the data means for Olist's strategic future. Direct the AI to think beyond the immediate patterns to their business implications.

MULTI-DIMENSIONAL ANALYSIS TECHNIQUES

Exploring the Olist Dataset from Multiple Angles

To extract maximum value from the Olist dataset, your prompts should encourage analysis across multiple dimensions simultaneously.

Key Analytical Dimensions

Temporal Analysis

  • Seasonal patterns
  • Day-of-week effects
  • Year-over-year trends
  • Before/after promotion events

Geographic Analysis

  • State and municipal patterns
  • Urban vs. rural differences
  • Distance effects (seller to customer)
  • Regional economic factors

Product Category Analysis

  • Category performance comparison
  • Cross-category purchasing
  • Category-specific satisfaction drivers
  • Price sensitivity by category

Stakeholder Analysis

  • Customer segments
  • Seller typologies
  • Logistics partner performance
  • Interaction effects between stakeholders

Multi-dimensional Prompt Example

Role: You are a multi-dimensional business analyst with expertise in identifying complex interaction effects in e-commerce ecosystems. Task: Analyze the Olist dataset to identify how different dimensions interact to create both opportunities and challenges for the marketplace. Approach: Conduct a multi-factorial analysis that examines the intersection of: 1. Time Factors: - Seasonal cycles - Day-of-week patterns - Time-of-day purchase patterns - Delivery time variations 2. Geographic Factors: - State and municipal differences - Urban vs. rural contrasts - Seller-customer distance effects - Regional economic indicators 3. Product Factors: - Category characteristics - Price point variations - Product size/weight logistics impacts - Category-specific review patterns 4. Behavioral Factors: - Customer purchasing patterns - Seller fulfillment behaviors - Payment method preferences - Review submission timing Output: Create a "Multi-dimensional Insight Matrix" that identifies: - The 5 most significant interaction effects between dimensions - How these interactions create both opportunities and challenges - Which interactions have the strongest impact on customer satisfaction - Which interactions have the strongest impact on marketplace efficiency For each key interaction, provide specific recommendations that leverage this understanding to improve Olist's performance.

Advanced Technique

When exploring multi-dimensional insights, guide the AI to look beyond simple correlations to identify conditional relationships. For example: "Product category A performs better in region X, but only during season Y, and primarily for customer segment Z."

Q&A AND WRAP-UP

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: How many prompts should we include in our final submission?

A: Quality over quantity! Focus on 3-7 well-crafted prompts that demonstrate progressive insight development rather than a large number of basic prompts.

Q: Can we use multiple AI tools or do we need to stick with one?

A: You can use any AI tools you prefer, and using multiple tools to compare outputs is encouraged. Just document which tools you used for which analyses.

Q: How will the judges evaluate the business value of our insights?

A: Judges will look for insights that are specific, actionable, supported by data, and connected to clear business outcomes for Olist. Quantifying potential impact is highly encouraged.

Q: Is it better to focus deeply on one business question or to address multiple questions?

A: It's generally better to develop deep, strategic insights around 1-2 focused business questions than to provide surface-level analysis across many questions.
