üéØ Your Mission

üíº You Are a Data Strategy Consultant

You've been hired by Airbnb's Boston Market Development team. As Boston debates new short-term rental regulations, Airbnb needs strategic insights to guide their response‚Äîinsights that balance business growth with community impact.

Your task: Not just to analyze data, but to craft AI prompts that reveal meaningful patterns and translate those patterns into strategic recommendations that serve multiple stakeholders.

This is prompt engineering in action: directing AI tools to uncover insights that would otherwise remain hidden in the complexity of thousands of listings, millions of reviews, and competing interests.

The Business Context

Boston's short-term rental market is at a crossroads. The city is considering stricter regulations amid concerns about:

  • Housing affordability and availability for residents
  • Neighborhood character and community cohesion
  • Hotel industry competition
  • Tax revenue and enforcement

Airbnb needs to understand their Boston market deeply to:

  1. Advocate effectively for balanced regulation
  2. Support successful hosting as a legitimate income source
  3. Improve guest experience and platform reputation
  4. Demonstrate community value beyond just economics
üí° The Challenge Within the Challenge

Different stakeholders have different definitions of success. Your analysis must acknowledge these competing interests and propose solutions that balance business growth with social responsibility.

The Dataset

You'll work with real Airbnb data from Boston (September 23, 2025), including:

  • ~4,500 listings across Boston neighborhoods
  • Listing details: pricing, property type, amenities, location coordinates
  • Host information: experience level, response rates, super-host status, number of properties
  • Review data: ratings, review text, frequency, guest comments
  • Availability data: booking patterns, occupancy trends
  • Neighborhood mapping: geographic distribution, proximity to landmarks

üé™ The Three Strategic Questions

‚ö†Ô∏è Critical Requirement

You must address ALL THREE questions in your analysis. Each question demonstrates different dimensions of AI-powered business intelligence and the SAIL framework.

Question 1
Host Success & Market Dynamics

"What factors distinguish successful hosts from struggling ones, and how can Airbnb help more hosts succeed?"

Context: Not all hosts are created equal. Some earn significant income with high ratings and consistent bookings. Others struggle with low occupancy and mediocre reviews. Understanding what drives success can help Airbnb design better host support programs.
Your Task:
  • Define what "host success" means (use multiple metrics, not just revenue)
  • Identify patterns that distinguish high-performing from low-performing hosts
  • Analyze whether success factors vary by property type, location, or host experience
  • Develop 3-5 actionable recommendations for host success programs
üí≠ Think About:
  • Are super-hosts fundamentally different, or do they just follow a playbook?
  • Does "professional" multi-property hosting outperform casual single-property hosts?
  • What role do pricing strategy, communication, and amenities play?
  • Are there hidden patterns in review sentiment that predict success?
Question 2
Neighborhood Impact & Market Saturation

"Which Boston neighborhoods show healthy market conditions vs. potential over-saturation, and what does this mean for Airbnb's growth strategy?"

Context: Airbnb faces criticism that short-term rentals harm residential neighborhoods by reducing housing availability and changing community character. Understanding neighborhood-level dynamics is critical for responsible growth.
Your Task:
  • Analyze Airbnb concentration across Boston neighborhoods
  • Identify patterns in pricing, listing types, and host behavior by neighborhood
  • Assess whether high-density areas show signs of market saturation or degraded quality
  • Determine which neighborhoods could sustainably support growth vs. which need intervention
üí≠ Think About:
  • Do tourist-heavy neighborhoods (Back Bay, North End) function differently than residential areas (Dorchester, Roslindale)?
  • Are entire-home listings pushing out long-term rentals in any neighborhoods?
  • How do review scores and pricing correlate with Airbnb density?
  • What patterns might suggest healthy market balance vs. harmful concentration?
Question 3
Guest Experience & Quality Assurance

"What drives exceptional guest experiences, and how can Airbnb identify and address quality issues before they damage platform reputation?"

Context: In a competitive market, guest satisfaction determines long-term success. Airbnb needs to understand what creates great experiences and develop early warning systems for quality problems.
Your Task:
  • Analyze the relationship between listing characteristics and guest satisfaction
  • Identify patterns in reviews that predict future quality issues
  • Develop criteria for "exceptional experience" vs. "problematic listing"
  • Propose a data-driven quality monitoring system
üí≠ Think About:
  • Beyond cleanliness and location, what drives high ratings?
  • Can you detect warning signs in review language before ratings drop?
  • Do certain host behaviors correlate with consistent guest satisfaction?
  • How should Airbnb balance strict quality control with supporting diverse listing types?

üß≠ The SAIL Framework in Action

üéØ What Makes SAIL Different

Your analysis should naturally demonstrate all four pillars of the SAIL framework. This isn't a checklist to force-fit‚Äîit's a way of thinking that distinguishes strategic AI users from mere tool operators.

The best submissions weave these elements together seamlessly, showing how technical skill, critical thinking, communication, and ethics reinforce each other.

S

Social Intelligence

"How do we connect?"

Translate technical findings into clear narratives for different audiences. Your final presentation must tell a compelling story, not just present data.

Like a translator bridging technical and non-technical worlds. The same insight needs completely different framing for Airbnb executives vs. city officials vs. hosts.
In Practice:
  • For Airbnb executives: Strategic recommendations with business impact
  • For hosts: Actionable guidance for improving performance
  • For city officials: Evidence-based insights on community impact
  • For guests: Quality indicators and value propositions
A

AI Literacy

"How does it work?"

Demonstrate sophisticated prompt engineering that shows you understand not just what to ask, but how and when different approaches work.

Like a conductor knowing which instrument to call upon at which moment in the symphony.
In Practice:
  • Use role-based prompting to analyze from different perspectives
  • Apply chain-of-thought reasoning for complex questions
  • Leverage multi-dimensional analysis (temporal, geographic, categorical)
  • Show iterative prompt refinement based on initial results
  • Document your prompts! We want to see your strategy.
I

Innovation/Inquiry

"What's missing or wrong?"

Go beyond obvious patterns. Question surprising correlations, challenge assumptions, look for what the data doesn't show, and consider alternative explanations.

Like a detective looking beyond the obvious explanations to find what's really happening.
In Practice:
  • When you find high revenue but low reviews‚Äîdig deeper
  • Question whether correlation implies causation
  • Identify potential biases or data limitations
  • Look for what's NOT in the data that should be
  • This is your "Wait, that's weird..." muscle. Use it!
L

Leadership

"Should we do this?"

Address ethical dimensions proactively. Consider impacts on different stakeholder groups and recognize that data-driven doesn't mean value-neutral.

Like a moral compass when technology outpaces ethics.
In Practice:
  • Consider impacts on hosts, guests, neighborhoods, and the city
  • Acknowledge tradeoffs in your recommendations
  • Think about unintended consequences
  • Propose solutions that balance competing interests
  • Don't just tell Airbnb what they want to hear‚Äîtell them what they need to know

Prompt Engineering Strategies

The Role-Based Approach ‚ñº

Instead of: "Analyze this data"

Try:

You are a market analyst for Airbnb. Analyze this Boston listings data to identify factors that distinguish successful hosts from struggling ones. Focus on patterns in pricing, ratings, reviews, and occupancy.

Why it works: Giving AI a role provides context and frames the analysis appropriately.

The Chain-of-Thought Approach ‚ñº

Instead of asking for a final answer, ask AI to think through steps:

Let's analyze host success in stages: Step 1: First, help me define what "successful host" means. What metrics should we consider beyond just revenue? Step 2: Once we have a definition, identify which variables in the dataset might correlate with success. Step 3: Analyze the top 10% of hosts by our success metric versus the bottom 10%. What patterns emerge? Step 4: Based on these patterns, what actionable recommendations would you make?

Why it works: Breaks complex questions into manageable pieces and shows your reasoning process.

The Multi-Perspective Approach ‚ñº
Analyze the concentration of Airbnb listings in Boston neighborhoods from three perspectives: 1. Airbnb's business perspective: Which neighborhoods offer growth opportunity? 2. A resident's perspective: Which neighborhoods might be experiencing over-saturation affecting housing availability? 3. A city planner's perspective: Which neighborhoods show healthy balance vs. concerning trends? For each perspective, identify 2-3 key insights and potential concerns.

Why it works: Demonstrates social intelligence and surfaces ethical considerations naturally.

The Constraint-Focused Approach ‚ñº
I need to identify high-quality Airbnb listings, but I want to avoid common pitfalls. Analyze the relationship between ratings and other factors, BUT specifically look for: 1. Listings with high ratings but suspiciously few reviews (might be new or fake) 2. Listings with high prices but mediocre ratings (might be overpriced) 3. Super-hosts with inconsistent review patterns (what makes them inconsistent?) Help me develop a more nuanced quality metric beyond just star ratings.

Why it works: Shows critical thinking and innovation/inquiry. You're not accepting surface-level metrics.
