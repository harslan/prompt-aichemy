UNDERSTANDING THE OLIST DATASET

Dataset Overview

The Brazilian E-Commerce Public Dataset by Olist contains information on 100,000 orders from 2016 to 2018 made across multiple marketplaces in Brazil.

Key Features:

  • Real commercial data (anonymized)
  • Multiple dimensions: orders, products, customers, sellers, reviews
  • Geographical information
  • Time-series data across two years

Data Source

This dataset is publicly available on Kaggle: https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/olistbr/brazilian-ecommerce

Data Schema

Files in the Dataset

  • olist_customers_dataset.csv - Customer information
  • olist_geolocation_dataset.csv - Geolocation data
  • olist_order_items_dataset.csv - Order items information
  • olist_order_payments_dataset.csv - Payment information
  • olist_order_reviews_dataset.csv - Customer reviews
  • olist_orders_dataset.csv - Order information
  • olist_products_dataset.csv - Product information
  • olist_sellers_dataset.csv - Seller information
  • product_category_name_translation.csv - Product category translations

PROMPT ENGINEERING FUNDAMENTALS

What is Prompt Engineering?

Prompt engineering is the strategic design of inputs to AI systems to guide them toward producing desired outputs with precision and relevance.

The Art & Science of Directing AI

Like a conductor directs musicians, effective prompts direct AI by providing:

  • Clear context and background
  • Specific instructions
  • Desired format and tone
  • Constraints and parameters
  • Examples when helpful

Why It Matters

The same AI model can produce dramatically different results based solely on how the prompt is engineered. Mastering this skill is about unlocking AI's potential to serve your specific business needs.

Key Elements of Effective Prompts

  1. Clarity: Be specific about what you want
  2. Context: Provide relevant background
  3. Constraints: Define boundaries and limitations
  4. Creativity: Allow room for AI to leverage its capabilities
  5. Criteria: Specify how to evaluate the response

Basic vs. Advanced Prompt Structure

Basic:
"Analyze the Olist customer data."

Advanced:
"As a business intelligence analyst for Olist, analyze the customer dataset to identify patterns in customer geographic distribution. Focus on the top 5 states by customer volume and visualize how customer density in these states has changed over the 2016-2018 period. Present the findings as a concise executive summary with 3 actionable recommendations for regional marketing strategy."

PROMPT ENGINEERING TECHNIQUES

Role-Based Prompting

Assign a specific role to the AI to frame its perspective and expertise.

"As a chief marketing officer for Olist, analyze the customer review data to identify opportunities for improving customer satisfaction..."

Chain of Thought

Guide the AI through a step-by-step reasoning process.

"First, identify the top-selling product categories. Then, analyze the correlation between price points and sales volume within these categories. Finally, recommend optimal pricing strategies."

Multi-perspective Analysis

Instruct the AI to examine data from different viewpoints.

"Analyze the delivery performance data from the perspective of customers, sellers, and logistics operators to identify common pain points."

Outcome-Focused Prompting

Clearly specify the desired outcome or deliverable format.

"Based on the Olist order data, create a one-page executive dashboard showing monthly sales trends, top-performing categories, and regional sales distribution."

Constraint Specification

Set clear boundaries and limitations for the analysis.

"Analyze customer satisfaction, focusing only on orders delivered within the last 30 days of the dataset and with complete review information."

Experimentation & Iteration

Refine prompts based on initial outputs to improve results.

"Based on the previous analysis of customer segments, further investigate the purchasing behavior of the high-value segment, specifically looking at product category preferences."

Pro Tip

For complex analyses, consider using a multi-prompt strategy—break down your investigation into sequential prompts, where each builds upon the insights from the previous one.

PRACTICAL PROMPT EXAMPLES

From Basic to Advanced for Olist Data Analysis

Basic Prompt

Analyze the Olist dataset and tell me about customer satisfaction.

Issues: Too vague, lacks context, no specific deliverable, no constraints

Intermediate Prompt

As a customer experience analyst, review the Olist order reviews dataset. Identify the main factors that contribute to low satisfaction scores (below 3). Present your findings with specific examples.

Improvements: Adds role context, specifies dataset, defines clear focus, requests examples

Advanced Prompt with AHES Framework

Role: As Olist's Chief Customer Officer responsible for improving customer experience across Brazil Task: Analyze the order reviews dataset in conjunction with order delivery data to identify: 1. The correlation between delivery time and review scores 2. Key themes in negative reviews (scores 1-2) using sentiment analysis 3. Geographic patterns in customer satisfaction Output Format: Prepare a strategic brief with: - 3 key findings with supporting data visualization - Root cause analysis for each issue identified - 2-3 actionable recommendations for each finding - Prioritized implementation roadmap based on potential business impact Constraints: - Focus on data from the last 6 months in the dataset - Consider both seller and logistics factors - Acknowledge limitations in the data and any assumptions made Ethical Considerations: - Ensure recommendations don't unfairly impact smaller sellers - Consider cultural and regional factors in customer expectations - Prioritize sustainable long-term solutions over quick fixes

AHES Elements:

  • AI Literacy: Specifies appropriate data sources and analysis types
  • Cognitive Elevation: Requests root cause analysis beyond simple correlations
  • Social Intelligence: Structures output for strategic business context
  • Ethical Leadership: Explicitly considers fairness and sustainability

BEST PRACTICES & TIPS FOR SUCCESS

Start with Exploration

Begin with broad, exploratory prompts to understand the dataset's potential before narrowing your focus.

"Provide a high-level summary of the Olist dataset, highlighting interesting patterns or anomalies worth investigating further."

Iterate & Refine

Treat prompt engineering as an iterative process—evaluate responses and adjust prompts accordingly.

"Based on the previous analysis of delivery delays, please dive deeper into the correlation between seller location and delivery performance."

Connect to Business Value

Always tie your analysis back to actionable business recommendations.

"For each insight identified, provide a specific business recommendation and estimate its potential impact on customer satisfaction and revenue."

Document Your Process

Keep a prompt journal to track what works, what doesn't, and how you've refined your approach.

Maintain a document with prompt versions, AI responses, and your evaluation of each iteration's effectiveness.

Think Multi-Dimensional

Examine the data from different stakeholder perspectives for a more comprehensive analysis.

"Analyze the same issue from the perspective of customers, sellers, and Olist's business operations."

Leverage Chain-of-Thought

Break complex analyses into sequential steps to guide the AI's reasoning process.

"First identify high-performing sellers, then analyze their characteristics, and finally develop a profile of ideal seller attributes."

Success Mindset

Think of yourself as an orchestrator—your role is to direct the AI's capabilities toward revealing meaningful insights, not to perform the analysis yourself. The quality of your prompts will determine the quality of the insights you uncover.
